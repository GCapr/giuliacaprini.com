Research

My research uses computational methods to examine how media content affects society, bridging political economy, health, behavioral, and cognitive economics.

Published

Does Candidates' Media Exposure Affect Vote Shares? Evidence From Pope Breaking News

Giulia Caprini

Journal of Public Economics, 2023

▶ Abstract
This paper examines whether brief media exposure affects political attitudes using data from the 2013 Italian election coinciding with Pope Benedict XVI's resignation. The temporary reduction in TV coverage of politician Berlusconi influenced voter behavior toward internet-based political news sources.

Working Papers

Visual Bias

[Submitted] - Best JM paper (Unicredit & EEA); "Marco Fanno" best paper award

Giulia Caprini

▶ Abstract
This paper analyzes approximately 300,000 photos from US news in 2020 using visual vocabulary and dictionary approaches. The findings show that media imagery is politically partisan and polarizing, and that partisan images influence reader opinions even when accompanying identical text. News' visual bias increases polarization, yet current fact-checking focuses primarily on text.

Work in Progress

The Noise Floor: Effort, Innovation, and Screening under AI-Mediated Peer Review

▶ Abstract
This project integrates LLMs into peer review and models screening under informed vs. uninformed review. The key concept is the "Noise Floor"—the irreducible rejection probability for high-quality work under uninformed review.

Strategic Storytelling

▶ Abstract
This research develops a framework for measuring "deceptiveness" as semantic distance between article previews and full content. Using a dataset of 200,000+ articles from 22 major US outlets (2020), it employs LLMs and vision models to identify "Trojan facts"—peripheral accurate facts strategically emphasized.
